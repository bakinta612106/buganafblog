Who was Stan Savran?

Stan Savran, originally known as Stanley George Savransky, was a highly respected media personality based in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania. His significant contributions to the sports industry earned him induction into the Western Pennsylvania Sports Hall of Fame and the Pittsburgh Pirates Media Wall of Fame.

Born on February 25, 1947, Savran began his career in radio after graduating from Miami University in Oxford, Ohio. He held various positions in Columbus, Lawton (OK), and Orlando (FL). In Orlando, he worked as a play-by-play announcer for the World Football League from 1974 to 1975.

In January 1976, Savran responded to a “blind” advertisement for a radio sportscaster in Broadcasting magazine, which marked his entry into the Pittsburgh sports scene. He initially worked at WWSW-AM but later moved to KQV when WWSW changed its format in 1979. From 1981 to 1991, he served as a dedicated sports reporter for WTAE-TV, delivering sports news from both the news desk and various locations.

Savran also hosted a talk show on WTAE Radio (now WPGP) from 8 to 9 p.m. during that time. This era, often referred to as the “Golden Era of Pittsburgh sports talk,” was notable for Savran’s exceptional skills and in-depth knowledge. After a disagreement with management, he left for KBL/Fox Sports Net.

From 2000 to 2004, Savran contributed weekly sports columns to the Pittsburgh Post-Gazette, the city’s largest newspaper. Throughout his career, he also served as a sports anchor for WTAE-TV and WPGH-TV in Pittsburgh. He hosted intermission segments during Pittsburgh Penguins broadcasts when the games aired on WPGH, and he continued to make occasional appearances in the same capacity for AT&T SportsNet Pittsburgh.
