Ice Spice Parents

Ice Spice, born in Isis Naija Gaston on January 1, 2000, in the Bronx, New York City, had a tumultuous upbringing that shaped her into the artist she is today.

Her father, Solomon Gaston, an African-American of Nigerian descent, was a former underground rapper, while her mother, Maria Gaston, a Dominican nurse, met and married Solomon when she was 17 and pregnant with Ice Spice.

However, their marriage ended when Isis was just two years old.

Growing up in the Fordham Road neighborhood of the Bronx exposed Isis to poverty, violence, and crime.

To provide a stable environment for her and her siblings, She spent much of her time with her grandparents and cousins, who supported her mother in raising the children.

It was during this time that her passion for hip hop took root, influenced by her father’s rap background and artists like Lil’ Kim, Nicki Minaj, and Jay-Z.

She began honing her skills in elementary school, writing poetry and freestyle raps, and adopted the stage name Ice Spice during her high school years.

Her father’s battle with Alzheimer’s disease ended in his passing in 2019.
