What Happened to Steve Hilton on Fox News?

Former Political advisor and TV host, Steve Hilton is one of the prominent personalities of America. He recently got bad news after Fox News announced to not air his show The Next Revolution.

British commentator Steve Hilton first joined Fox News Media in last 2016 and he is currently serving as a contributor.

In addition to Hilton’s role at Fox News Channel, he is the co-founder of Crowpac and he is also a teacher at Stanford University’s Institute of Design. He is also the author of the bestseller book, “More Human: Designing a World Where People Come First.” and also a political commentator.

Steve Hilton previously served as a senior adviser to former United Kingdom Prime Minister David Cameron.

What Happened to the Next Revolution With Steve Hilton?

Steve Hilton, 53, hosted the Next Revolution on Sundays at 9 p.m. since 2017. On 1 June, Fox News announced that the Sunday program Next Revolution will no more airing on Fox News Channel. Meanwhile, it is also announced that he will remain with the channel as a contributor.

Former United States Representative Trey Gowdy hosted Fox’s 7 p.m. hour on Sunday with Sunday Night in America. Now, he took Steve’s 9 p.m. timeslot.
