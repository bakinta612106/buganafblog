It appears Chicago Bears wide receiver DJ Moore is not happy about being snubbed from the 2024 NFL Pro Bowl Games roster.

After the NFL announced the rosters on Wednesday night, Moore posted on X, formerly known as Twitter:

DJ Moore @idjmoore

Los Angeles Rams rookie Puka Nacua, Tampa Bay Buccaneers veteran Mike Evans, Philadelphia Eagles star A.J. Brown and Dallas Cowboys standout CeeDee Lamb were the four NFC wide receivers chosen to participate in the Pro Bowl Games.

Moore has never earned a Pro Bowl selection in his six-year career, though his performance this season was certainly worthy of a nod. In 16 games, he has caught 92 passes for 1,300 yards and eight touchdowns.

The 26-year-old's 1,300 receiving yards ranks seventh in the NFL and sixth in the NFC. Nacua, Brown and Lamb have recorded more receiving yards this season, but Evans ranks behind Moore with 1,233 yards.

However, Evans has also caught 13 touchdown passes this season, which ranks first in the NFL. Moore's eight touchdown catches rank eighth in the league.

While Moore was snubbed, the Bears had two players earn Pro Bowl honors this seasonâ€”edge rusher Montez Sweat and cornerback Jaylon Johnson.

Sweat, who began the season with the Washington Commanders, has posted 12.5 sacks, four passes defended, three forced fumbles, 54 tackles, 13 tackles for loss and 25 quarterback hits in 16 games.

The 27-year-old has been particularly impressive in Chicago, posting six sacks, three passes defended, one forced fumble, 22 tackles, three tackles for loss and 14 quarterback hits in eight games.

Johnson, in his fourth NFL season, has posted four interceptions, one of which was returned for a touchdown, one forced fumble, 36 tackles and one tackle for loss in 14 games.

The 2024 NFL Pro Bowl Games are set for Feb. 4 at Camping World Stadium in Orlando, Florida.
